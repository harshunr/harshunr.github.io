Publications

IEEE

Activity-Aware Data Rate Tuning in Wireless Body Area Networks

This study introduces an innovative approach called Activity-Aware Data Rate Tuning (A2D) for Wireless Body Area Network (WBAN), with a focus on the criticality of sensed physiological data. We take into account various physical activities of patients, evaluating their health criticality. Subsequently, the data rate of physiological sensors is adjusted based on these criticality values. Since the readings from physiological sensors may vary depending on a person's physical activity, it is crucial for a WBAN to be activity-aware to accurately assess criticality values. The proposed scheme was implemented on a real hardware platform to demonstrate its effectiveness.

A Computer Vision Framework for Partitioning of Image-Object Through Graph Theoretical Heuristic Approach

The objective of this study is to create a graph-theoretical computer vision framework for segmenting the shape of an image object into parts, using a heuristic approach that aligns with human perception. The proposed framework utilizes a specialized polygonal approximation scheme to represent a shape in a simplified graph form, where each side of the polygon corresponds to a graph edge. A vertex-visibility graph is derived from the shape-representative graph using a straightforward algorithm introduced in this paper. We introduce an iterative clique extraction strategy based on heuristics to decompose the shape-representative graph, relying on its vertex-visibility graph. To evaluate the proposed framework, we employ the MPEG-7 shape dataset, and our observations indicate that its performance is comparable to existing schemes.
